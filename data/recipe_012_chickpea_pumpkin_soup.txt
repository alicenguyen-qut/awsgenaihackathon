Recipe: Chickpea Pumpkin Soup

Recipe_ID: DA_CHK_PUMPKIN_001
Author: Fran Foulkes-Taylor, Accredited Practising Dietitian (APD)
Source: Dietitians Australia
URL: https://dietitiansaustralia.org.au/recipes/chickpea-pumpkin-soup
Reviewed_Date: 21 Feb 2022

Category: Soup / Main Course
Cuisine: Australian / Mediterranean-inspired
Difficulty: Easy
Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cooking Time: 35 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes
Servings: 4-6 people

Description:
A warming, creamy soup featuring roasted pumpkin and protein-rich chickpeas. This nutritious vegetarian soup provides balanced macronutrients with plant-based protein and fibre, plus abundant micronutrients from pumpkin. Perfect for a comforting meal that supports healthy eating principles.

Ingredients:
- 1/4 large Kent pumpkin (approximately 1kg, peeled and cubed)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 teaspoons cumin
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 garlic cloves, peeled and crushed
- 3 cups salt-reduced vegetable stock
- 1 tin (400g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 tin (375ml) evaporated milk
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: toasted wholemeal bread for serving

Cooking Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 190°C (375°F).
2. Spread pumpkin cubes on a baking tray in a single layer.
3. Drizzle with 1 tablespoon olive oil and toss to coat evenly.
4. Roast pumpkin for about 30 minutes until soft and lightly caramelized.
5. While pumpkin roasts, heat remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
6. Add diced onion and cook for approximately 2 minutes until softened.
7. Add crushed garlic and cumin; cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
8. Add vegetable stock and drained chickpeas to the pot.
9. Bring to a simmer and cook on low heat for 10 minutes.
10. Add the roasted pumpkin to the pot.
11. Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth and creamy (or transfer to a blender in batches).
12. Gradually add evaporated milk while blending to achieve desired creamy consistency.
13. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
14. Serve hot, optionally with toasted wholemeal bread on the side.

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: vegetarian diets, gluten-free diets (serve with GF bread if needed), balanced nutrition, family-friendly meals.
Allergens: Dairy (evaporated milk)
This recipe is NOT suitable for: vegan (contains dairy - unless modified), dairy-free (unless modified).

Adaptations:
- Vegan/Dairy-free: Replace evaporated milk with coconut milk or unsweetened plant-based milk
- Extra protein: Add more chickpeas or serve with a dollop of Greek yogurt
- Spicier version: Add chili flakes or cayenne pepper
- Creamier texture: Add extra evaporated milk or coconut cream
- Lower sodium: Use homemade stock instead of store-bought

Health Benefits:
- High in plant-based protein and fibre from chickpeas
- Rich in beta-carotene and vitamin A from pumpkin
- Supports immune function and eye health
- Good source of complex carbohydrates for sustained energy
- Contains healthy unsaturated fats from olive oil
- Anti-inflammatory properties from cumin and garlic
- Supports digestive health with high fibre content
- Low in saturated fat

Nutrition Expert Notes:
This soup provides balanced macronutrients with a focus on plant-based protein and fibre from chickpeas, and vegetable micronutrients from pumpkin. Using salt-reduced stock supports sodium control aligned with healthy eating principles. For dairy-free alternatives, substitute evaporated milk with unsweetened plant-based milk and adjust seasoning.

Ingredient Substitutions:
- Use butternut squash instead of Kent pumpkin
- Replace chickpeas with white beans or lentils
- Use coconut milk for a tropical twist
- Add ginger for extra warmth and flavor
- Include carrots or sweet potato for more vegetables
- Top with toasted pumpkin seeds for crunch
