Recipe: Mushroom Shakshuka

Category: Main Course
Cuisine: Middle Eastern / Mediterranean
Difficulty: Medium
Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cooking Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 3 people

Recipe ID: DA_MUSHROOM_SHAKSHUKA_004
Author: Lyndi Cohen, Accredited Practising Dietitian (APD)
Source: Dietitians Australia — Mushroom shakshuka
URL: https://dietitiansaustralia.org.au/recipes/mushroom-shakshuka
Reviewed Date: 21 Feb 2022

Description:
A vibrant vegetarian shakshuka featuring mushrooms, capsicum, and eggs poached in a rich tomato sauce with harissa spice. This nutrient-dense meal combines colorful vegetables and mushrooms for fiber, micronutrients, and antioxidants, while eggs provide high-quality protein and essential nutrients like choline. Perfect for breakfast, brunch, or dinner.

Ingredients:
- 1 small red onion, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 2 cups mushrooms, finely sliced
- 2 large red or yellow capsicum, cut into 0.5 cm cubes
- 4 cloves garlic, crushed
- 2-4 tablespoons harissa paste (to preference)
- 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- 1.5 teaspoons ground cumin
- 0.5 teaspoon dried chilli flakes (optional)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 5 large ripe tomatoes (800g) or 2 x 400g tins chopped peeled tomatoes
- 3 cups baby spinach leaves
- 4-6 free-range eggs
- Small handful of coriander, roughly chopped

Cooking Instructions:
1. Heat olive oil in a large frying pan over medium heat and cook the onion until softened, approximately 4-5 minutes.
2. Add half the mushrooms and cook for 2-3 minutes until they begin to soften.
3. Add the remaining mushrooms, capsicum, tomato paste, harissa, ground cumin, chilli flakes (if using), salt, pepper, and garlic. Cook for approximately 5 minutes, adding a splash of water if the mixture becomes too thick.
4. Add tomatoes and spinach, then cook for approximately 10 minutes until the sauce thickens and reduces.
5. Create small wells in the sauce and carefully crack eggs into them.
6. Simmer for 8-10 minutes until egg whites are firm and yolks are cooked to your preference.
7. Garnish with roughly chopped coriander and serve immediately.

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: vegetarian diets, gluten-free diets (confirm all ingredient brands), high-protein diets, Mediterranean diet.
This recipe is NOT suitable for: vegan diets (contains eggs), egg-free diets.
Allergens: Contains eggs. May contain traces of allergens depending on harissa brand.
Halal: Likely suitable (confirm egg sourcing if strict halal compliance is needed).

Health Benefits:
- High in dietary fiber (13g per serving) supporting digestive health
- Excellent source of protein from eggs for muscle maintenance
- Rich in antioxidants from tomatoes, capsicum, and spinach
- Contains heart-healthy monounsaturated fats from olive oil
- Provides essential vitamins A, C, and K from vegetables
- Good source of choline from eggs, important for brain health
- Anti-inflammatory properties from olive oil and vegetables
- Supports immune function with vitamin C-rich vegetables

Ingredient Substitutions:
- For vegan/dairy-free: Replace eggs with firm tofu cubes or chickpeas (nutritional profile will change)
- Reduce or omit harissa for lower spice/salt content
- Use fresh tomatoes or canned tomatoes interchangeably
- Substitute kale or Swiss chard for spinach
- Add chickpeas for extra plant-based protein
- Use any mushroom variety (button, Swiss brown, portobello)

Meal Timing: Suitable for breakfast, brunch, lunch, or dinner
Season: All seasons, especially comforting in autumn and winter

Nutrition Expert Notes:
This mushroom shakshuka aligns with the Australian Dietary Guidelines promoting vegetable-rich meals with plant foods, lean protein (eggs), and balanced macronutrients as part of a wholesome main dish. The recipe is particularly high in fiber and provides a good balance of protein and healthy fats. Using olive oil supports cardiovascular health. For those sensitive to spice, reduce or omit harissa. For plant-based variations, replace eggs with firm tofu cubes and increase legumes for protein. Always confirm ingredient brands meet specific dietary restrictions before including in meal plans.
