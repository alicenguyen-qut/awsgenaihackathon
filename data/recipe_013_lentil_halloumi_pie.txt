Recipe: Lentil and Halloumi Pie

Recipe_ID: DA_LENTIL_HALLOUMI_PIE_003
Author: Vanessa Schuldt, Accredited Practising Dietitian (APD)
Source: Dietitians Australia
URL: https://dietitiansaustralia.org.au/recipes/lentil-and-haloumi-pie
Reviewed_Date: 20 Feb 2024

Category: Main Course / Pie
Cuisine: Mediterranean-inspired
Difficulty: Medium
Preparation Time: 20 minutes
Cooking Time: 40 minutes
Total Time: 60 minutes
Servings: 4 people

Description:
A hearty vegetarian pie featuring protein-rich brown lentils topped with creamy mashed potato and golden halloumi cheese. This comforting dish combines plant-based protein with vegetables and dairy, creating a balanced meal that's perfect for family dinners.

Ingredients:
- 800g Desiree potatoes, peeled and diced
- 30g olive oil margarine spread
- 1/2 cup (125ml) lite milk
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1 garlic clove, peeled and crushed
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 medium carrots, chopped small
- 2 × 400g cans brown lentils, rinsed and drained
- 400g can diced tomatoes (with basil and oregano)
- 180g halloumi cheese, cut into 1cm-thick slices
- Salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 200°C (fan forced) or 220°C (conventional).
2. Steam potatoes in a steamer or pot until tender (about 15-20 minutes).
3. Mash potatoes well with margarine and lite milk until smooth and creamy.
4. Season mashed potato with salt and pepper to taste; keep warm and set aside.
5. While potatoes cook, heat extra virgin olive oil in a heavy-based pan over medium heat.
6. Add crushed garlic, chopped onion, and chopped carrots to the pan.
7. Cook for approximately 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender.
8. Add rinsed and drained brown lentils and canned diced tomatoes to the pan.
9. Stir well and cook for about 10 minutes until the sauce thickens and reduces slightly.
10. Spoon the lentil mixture into a 6-cup capacity ovenproof dish, spreading evenly.
11. Top the lentil mixture with the mashed potatoes, spreading to cover completely.
12. Press halloumi slices into the mashed potato topping, arranging evenly across the surface.
13. Bake in preheated oven for 20-25 minutes until the top is golden brown and halloumi is slightly crispy.
14. Remove from oven, let rest for 5 minutes, then serve hot.

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: vegetarian diets, high-protein diets, high-fibre diets, family-friendly meals.
Allergens: Dairy (halloumi cheese, milk, margarine)
This recipe is NOT suitable for: vegan (contains dairy), dairy-free (unless modified), low-sodium diets (high sodium content).

Adaptations:
- Vegan/Dairy-free: Use plant-based margarine, plant milk, and vegan cheese alternative
- Lower sodium: Use low-salt canned tomatoes, rinse lentils thoroughly, reduce halloumi or use lower-salt cheese
- Gluten-free: Confirm all ingredient brands are gluten-free certified
- Extra vegetables: Add zucchini, mushrooms, or spinach to lentil mixture
- Lower fat: Use reduced-fat halloumi and skim milk

Health Benefits:
- Excellent source of plant-based protein from lentils
- Very high in dietary fibre for digestive health (13g per serving)
- Rich in complex carbohydrates for sustained energy
- Good source of calcium from dairy products
- Contains beta-carotene from carrots
- Provides iron and folate from lentils
- Supports muscle maintenance and satiety with high protein content

Nutrition Expert Notes:
This pie leverages lentils for plant protein and fibre, with halloumi adding texture and calcium. Using lighter milk and olive oil supports healthier fat quality. For a lower sodium variant, use low-salt canned tomatoes and rinse lentils thoroughly. Swapping halloumi for a lower-salt cheese or vegan alternative can further reduce saturated fat and sodium.

Ingredient Substitutions:
- Use sweet potato instead of regular potato for extra nutrients
- Replace halloumi with feta or mozzarella cheese
- Add red lentils for extra protein and creamier texture
- Use fresh tomatoes instead of canned for lower sodium
- Include herbs like thyme or rosemary for extra flavor
- Top with breadcrumbs for extra crunch

Meal Timing: Suitable for lunch, dinner, family meals, meal prep
Season: All seasons, especially comforting in autumn and winter
