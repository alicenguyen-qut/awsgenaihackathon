Recipe: Creamy Pea Pasta

Recipe_ID: DA_CREAMY_PEA_PASTA_002
Author: Amanda Maiorano, Accredited Practising Dietitian (APD)
Source: Dietitians Australia
URL: https://dietitiansaustralia.org.au/recipes/creamy-pea-pasta
Reviewed_Date: 16 Dec 2022

Category: Main Course
Cuisine: Italian-inspired
Difficulty: Easy
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 15 minutes
Total Time: 25 minutes
Servings: 4 people

Description:
A creamy, vibrant pasta dish featuring a smooth pea and basil sauce. This vegetarian recipe incorporates legumes to boost fibre, plant protein, and micronutrient intake while using olive oil for healthy unsaturated fats. Perfect for a quick weeknight dinner that's both nutritious and delicious.

Ingredients:
- 400g short dried pasta (orecchiette recommended)
- 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 cups frozen peas (280g), defrosted
- 1/4 cup fresh basil
- 1/2 cup pasta cooking water
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon lemon zest
- 1/2 cup grated parmesan or pecorino cheese
- Salt and black pepper, to season

Cooking Instructions:
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil and cook pasta until al dente according to package directions.
2. While pasta cooks, add olive oil to a medium saucepan and cook garlic for 2-3 minutes, or until soft but still blonde (not browned).
3. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta cooking water before draining.
4. To a blender, add the cooked garlic and oil, 1.5 cups of the defrosted peas, fresh basil, lemon juice, salt, black pepper, and 1/2 cup of pasta water.
5. Blend until smooth and creamy, creating a vibrant green sauce.
6. Return the sauce to the pan over low heat.
7. Add the cooked pasta to the sauce and stir until well-coated, adding more pasta water if needed to reach desired consistency.
8. Add the grated cheese, remaining 1/2 cup peas, and lemon zest.
9. Stir everything together until well combined.
10. Serve immediately while hot, with extra parmesan if desired.

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: vegetarian diets, balanced nutrition, family-friendly meals.
Allergens: Gluten (pasta), Dairy (cheese)
This recipe is NOT suitable for: vegan (contains dairy), gluten-free (unless modified with GF pasta), dairy-free (unless modified).

Adaptations:
- Gluten-free: Use gluten-free pasta
- Dairy-free/Vegan: Replace cream with plant-based alternative and use dairy-free cheese substitute or nutritional yeast
- Extra nutrition: Use wholemeal pasta to increase fibre content
- Lower fat: Reduce cheese quantity to manage saturated fat
- More vegetables: Add spinach, zucchini, or broccoli for extra nutrients

Health Benefits:
- High in plant protein from peas and legumes
- Good source of dietary fibre for digestive health
- Contains unsaturated fats from olive oil supporting heart health
- Rich in vitamins A, C, and K from peas and basil
- Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy
- Antioxidants from fresh herbs and vegetables
- Supports healthy eating principles per Australian Dietary Guidelines

Nutrition Expert Notes:
This recipe aligns with healthy eating principles by incorporating legumes (peas) to increase fibre, plant protein, and micronutrient intake. Using olive oil supports inclusion of unsaturated fats. To improve nutritional balance further, consider:
- Using wholemeal pasta to increase fibre
- Reducing added cheese to manage saturated fat
- Adding extra vegetables (spinach, zucchini, broccoli) to increase volume and micronutrient density

Ingredient Substitutions:
- Use any short pasta shape (penne, fusilli, shells)
- Replace peas with edamame or broad beans
- Add baby spinach for extra greens
- Use mint instead of basil for different flavor
- Add pine nuts or walnuts for extra texture and healthy fats
