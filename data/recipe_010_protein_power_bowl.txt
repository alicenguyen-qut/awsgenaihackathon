Recipe: Protein Power Bowl

Category: Main Course
Cuisine: American / Fitness
Difficulty: Easy
Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cooking Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 2 people

Description:
A nutrient-dense power bowl designed for muscle growth and recovery, featuring multiple protein sources, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats. This complete meal provides sustained energy and supports athletic performance.

Ingredients:
- 200g chicken breast, grilled and sliced
- 150g quinoa (uncooked)
- 100g chickpeas, cooked
- 2 hard-boiled eggs
- 100g sweet potato, cubed and roasted
- 80g avocado, sliced
- 60g edamame, steamed
- 50g mixed greens
- 2 tablespoons tahini dressing
- 1 tablespoon pumpkin seeds
- Salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Instructions:
1. Cook quinoa according to package directions and set aside.
2. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
3. Toss sweet potato cubes with a little oil, salt, and pepper, roast for 20-25 minutes.
4. Season and grill chicken breast for 6-8 minutes per side, then slice.
5. Boil eggs for 10 minutes, cool, peel, and halve.
6. Steam edamame for 5 minutes.
7. In a large bowl, arrange quinoa as the base.
8. Top with grilled chicken, chickpeas, eggs, sweet potato, edamame, and greens.
9. Add sliced avocado and drizzle with tahini dressing.
10. Sprinkle pumpkin seeds on top and serve.

Nutritional Information (per serving):
- Calories: 680 kcal
- Protein: 52g
- Carbohydrates: 64g
- Fat: 24g
- Fiber: 14g
- Sodium: 420mg

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: high-protein diets, muscle-building diets, balanced nutrition, gluten-free, dairy-free.
Allergens: Eggs, sesame (tahini), soy (edamame)
This recipe is NOT suitable for: low-carb diets, vegan (contains eggs and chicken).

Health Benefits:
- Extremely high in protein for muscle growth and repair
- Complete amino acid profile from multiple protein sources
- Complex carbohydrates for sustained energy
- Healthy fats from avocado and seeds support hormone production
- High in fiber for digestive health
- Rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants
- Supports post-workout recovery

Ingredient Substitutions:
- Replace chicken with salmon or turkey
- Use brown rice instead of quinoa
- Substitute tahini with Greek yogurt dressing
- Add roasted broccoli or Brussels sprouts
- Use sunflower seeds instead of pumpkin seeds
- Replace eggs with extra chickpeas for vegetarian option

Meal Timing: Suitable for lunch, dinner, post-workout meal
Season: All seasons
