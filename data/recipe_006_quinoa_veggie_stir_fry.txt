Recipe: Quinoa Veggie Stir-Fry

Category: Main Course
Cuisine: Asian Fusion
Difficulty: Easy
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 2 people

Description:
A colorful and nutritious plant-based stir-fry featuring protein-rich quinoa and a variety of fresh vegetables. This complete protein source is perfect for vegetarians and vegans looking for a satisfying, nutrient-dense meal.

Ingredients:
- 200g quinoa (uncooked)
- 150g broccoli florets
- 100g bell peppers (mixed colors)
- 100g snap peas
- 80g carrots, julienned
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

Cooking Instructions:
1. Rinse quinoa and cook according to package directions (usually 15 minutes in boiling water).
2. While quinoa cooks, chop all vegetables into bite-sized pieces.
3. Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat.
4. Add garlic and ginger, stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add carrots and broccoli, stir-fry for 3 minutes.
6. Add bell peppers and snap peas, cook for another 2-3 minutes.
7. Add cooked quinoa to the wok and mix well.
8. Pour soy sauce and sesame oil over the mixture, toss to combine.
9. Garnish with sesame seeds before serving.

Nutritional Information (per serving):
- Calories: 450 kcal
- Protein: 16g
- Carbohydrates: 62g
- Fat: 16g
- Fiber: 10g
- Sodium: 680mg

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: vegetarian, vegan, high-fiber diets, plant-based diets, dairy-free diets.
Allergens: Soy, sesame
This recipe is NOT suitable for: gluten-free (due to soy sauce - use tamari instead), low-carb diets.

Health Benefits:
- Complete protein source from quinoa
- High in fiber for digestive health
- Rich in vitamins A, C, and K from vegetables
- Contains antioxidants from colorful vegetables
- Supports heart health with plant-based nutrients

Ingredient Substitutions:
- Use tamari instead of soy sauce for gluten-free option
- Replace quinoa with brown rice or cauliflower rice
- Add tofu or edamame for extra protein
- Use any seasonal vegetables available

Meal Timing: Suitable for lunch, dinner
Season: All seasons
