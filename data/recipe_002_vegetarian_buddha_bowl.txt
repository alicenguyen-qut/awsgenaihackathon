Recipe: Vegetarian Buddha Bowl

Category: Main Course
Cuisine: Asian Fusion
Difficulty: Medium
Preparation Time: 20 minutes
Cooking Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 2 people

Description:
A colorful and nutritious vegetarian Buddha bowl packed with plant-based protein from quinoa and chickpeas, roasted sweet potatoes, massaged kale, creamy avocado, and a tangy tahini dressing. This complete meal provides all essential nutrients and is perfect for vegans and vegetarians seeking a balanced, fiber-rich meal.

Ingredients:
- 150g quinoa (dry)
- 200g chickpeas (canned, drained)
- 200g sweet potato
- 100g kale
- 1 medium avocado
- 3 tablespoons tahini
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 clove garlic
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- Salt and spices to taste

Cooking Instructions:
1. Rinse quinoa and cook according to package instructions (typically 15 minutes in boiling water).
2. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F).
3. Cube the sweet potato into bite-sized pieces and spread on a baking sheet.
4. Roast sweet potato for 20 minutes until tender and slightly caramelized.
5. Drain and rinse the chickpeas, then toss with olive oil and your choice of spices (cumin, paprika work well).
6. Remove stems from kale and massage leaves with a pinch of salt until softened.
7. Make the tahini dressing by whisking together tahini, lemon juice, minced garlic, and water until smooth.
8. Arrange cooked quinoa, roasted sweet potato, seasoned chickpeas, and massaged kale in bowls.
9. Top with sliced avocado and drizzle generously with tahini dressing.

Nutritional Information (per serving):
- Calories: 520 kcal
- Protein: 18g
- Carbohydrates: 62g
- Fat: 22g
- Fiber: 15g
- Sodium: 320mg

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: vegetarian diets, vegan diets, high-fiber diets, gluten-free diets, dairy-free diets, plant-based diets.
Allergens: Contains sesame (tahini)
This recipe is NOT suitable for: sesame allergies, low-carb diets.

Health Benefits:
- High in plant-based protein from quinoa and chickpeas
- Rich in complex carbohydrates for sustained energy
- Supports digestive health with 15g of fiber per serving
- Contains healthy fats from avocado and tahini
- Rich in vitamins A, C, and K from kale and sweet potato
- Anti-inflammatory properties from whole plant foods

Ingredient Substitutions:
- Replace quinoa with brown rice or farro
- Use white beans instead of chickpeas
- Substitute butternut squash for sweet potato
- Replace tahini with almond butter or cashew cream
- Use spinach instead of kale

Meal Timing: Suitable for lunch, dinner, meal prep
Season: All seasons, especially autumn with roasted vegetables
