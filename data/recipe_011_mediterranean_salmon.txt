Recipe: Mediterranean Salmon

Category: Main Course
Cuisine: Mediterranean
Difficulty: Easy
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 2 people

Description:
A heart-healthy Mediterranean-style baked salmon featuring omega-3 rich fish topped with fresh tomatoes, olives, and herbs. This dish is perfect for supporting cardiovascular health while providing high-quality protein.

Ingredients:
- 2 salmon fillets (about 300g total)
- 150g cherry tomatoes, halved
- 50g Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 lemon (juice and zest)
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 100g baby spinach (for serving)

Cooking Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Place salmon fillets in a baking dish, season with salt and pepper.
3. In a bowl, mix cherry tomatoes, olives, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, and half the fresh herbs.
4. Spoon the tomato mixture over and around the salmon.
5. Drizzle with a little extra olive oil and sprinkle lemon zest on top.
6. Bake for 15-18 minutes until salmon is cooked through and flakes easily.
7. While salmon bakes, lightly sauté spinach in a pan until wilted.
8. Serve salmon over spinach, garnished with remaining fresh herbs.
9. Drizzle pan juices over the top.

Nutritional Information (per serving):
- Calories: 420 kcal
- Protein: 38g
- Carbohydrates: 12g
- Fat: 26g
- Fiber: 4g
- Sodium: 520mg
- Omega-3: 2.5g

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: heart-healthy diets, Mediterranean diet, high-protein diets, low-carb diets, keto-friendly, gluten-free, dairy-free, paleo-friendly.
Allergens: Fish
This recipe is NOT suitable for: vegetarian, vegan, pescatarian who avoid fish.

Health Benefits:
- Rich in omega-3 fatty acids for heart health
- Supports brain function and cognitive health
- Anti-inflammatory properties from salmon and olive oil
- High-quality protein for muscle maintenance
- Antioxidants from tomatoes and herbs
- Supports healthy cholesterol levels
- May reduce risk of cardiovascular disease

Ingredient Substitutions:
- Use trout or arctic char instead of salmon
- Replace olives with capers for different flavor
- Add artichoke hearts for extra Mediterranean flair
- Use basil instead of dill
- Serve with quinoa or brown rice for extra carbs
- Add feta cheese for extra flavor (if not dairy-free)

Meal Timing: Suitable for lunch, dinner
Season: All seasons
