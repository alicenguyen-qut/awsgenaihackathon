Recipe: Zesty Thai Noodle Salad

Recipe_ID: DA_ZESTY_THAI_NOODLE_SALAD_005
Author: Lyndi Cohen, Accredited Practising Dietitian (APD)
Source: Dietitians Australia
URL: https://dietitiansaustralia.org.au/recipes/zesty-thai-noodle-salad
Reviewed_Date: 20 Feb 2022

Category: Salad / Main Course
Cuisine: Thai-inspired
Difficulty: Easy
Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cooking Time: 5 minutes
Total Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 8 people

Description:
A vibrant, fresh Thai-inspired noodle salad packed with colorful vegetables, herbs, and a zesty homemade dressing. This light yet satisfying dish is perfect for meal prep, picnics, or as a refreshing lunch option. High in vegetables and fiber with a balanced nutritional profile.

Ingredients:

Salad:
- 1/2 cup edamame beans (fresh or frozen)
- 1 packet vermicelli rice noodles (approximately 200g)
- 1 coleslaw mix (cabbage and carrot, leave out dressing)
- 1 packet mixed salad leaves
- 1 cup snow peas, sliced
- 1 red capsicum (bell pepper), sliced
- 2 spring onions, chopped
- 1 avocado, sliced
- Large handful fresh coriander, chopped (with stalks)
- Large handful fresh mint, leaves removed from stems
- 1/2 cup bean shoots

Salad Dressing:
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon rice wine vinegar
- 1 tablespoon sweet chilli sauce
- 1 tablespoon coconut sugar (or regular sugar)
- Juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 1/2 teaspoon fish sauce (optional - omit for vegetarian/vegan)

Cooking Instructions:
1. Pour boiling water over rice noodles and edamame beans in a large bowl.
2. Let sit for approximately 5 minutes until noodles are soft and edamame is cooked.
3. Drain well and rinse with cold water to stop cooking process.
4. In a large serving bowl, combine cooked noodles and edamame.
5. Add coleslaw mix, mixed salad leaves, sliced snow peas, sliced red capsicum, and chopped spring onions.
6. Add sliced avocado, chopped coriander (including stalks), and mint leaves.
7. Toss all ingredients together gently.
8. In a jar with a lid, combine all dressing ingredients: soy sauce, sesame oil, rice wine vinegar, sweet chilli sauce, coconut sugar, lemon juice, olive oil, and fish sauce (if using).
9. Shake jar vigorously until dressing is well combined and emulsified.
10. Pour dressing over the salad and toss well to coat all ingredients evenly.
11. Top with fresh bean shoots.
12. Serve immediately or refrigerate for up to 2 hours before serving.

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: vegetarian diets (if fish sauce omitted), vegan diets (if fish sauce omitted), gluten-free diets (use GF soy sauce), dairy-free, plant-based diets.
Allergens: Soy (soy sauce), sesame (sesame oil), fish (fish sauce - optional)
This recipe is NOT suitable for: nut-free diets (check if processed in facility with nuts), low-sodium diets (adjust soy sauce).

Adaptations:
- Vegan/Vegetarian: Omit fish sauce or replace with extra soy sauce
- Gluten-free: Use tamari or gluten-free soy sauce
- Lower sodium: Use reduced-sodium soy sauce
- Extra protein: Add grilled chicken, prawns, or tofu
- Nut-free: Ensure all ingredients are processed in nut-free facilities
- Spicier: Add fresh chili or extra sweet chilli sauce

Health Benefits:
- High in fresh vegetables providing vitamins and minerals
- Good source of dietary fibre for digestive health
- Contains plant-based protein from edamame
- Rich in antioxidants from colorful vegetables and herbs
- Healthy fats from avocado and olive oil
- Low in saturated fat
- Provides vitamin C from capsicum and herbs
- Supports hydration with high water content vegetables

Nutrition Expert Notes:
This salad is high in fresh vegetables and herbs, offering fibre and micronutrients with a light, balanced profile. Rice noodles and edamame contribute energy and plant protein, while the dressing adds flavour. Adjust fish sauce/soy sauce to manage sodium. Confirm brands to ensure dietary requirements (gluten, vegan) are met.

Ingredient Substitutions:
- Use soba noodles or glass noodles instead of rice noodles
- Replace edamame with chickpeas or green peas
- Add shredded chicken or prawns for extra protein
- Use lime juice instead of lemon juice
- Add crushed peanuts for extra crunch (if not nut-free)
- Include cucumber or radish for extra crunch
- Use Thai basil instead of regular mint
