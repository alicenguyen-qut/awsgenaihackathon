Recipe: Salmon with Roasted Vegetables

Category: Main Course
Cuisine: Nordic
Difficulty: Easy
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 35 minutes
Servings: 2 people

Description:
A simple yet elegant Nordic-inspired dish featuring omega-3 rich salmon fillet roasted alongside colorful vegetables including broccoli, bell pepper, and zucchini. This one-pan meal is perfect for busy weeknights and provides exceptional nutritional benefits for brain health and inflammation reduction.

Ingredients:
- 300g salmon fillet (fresh or thawed)
- 200g broccoli florets
- 1 large bell pepper (any color)
- 1 medium zucchini
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 tablespoon fresh dill (or 1 teaspoon dried)
- 1 whole lemon
- Salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Instructions:
1. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F).
2. Chop the broccoli into florets, slice the bell pepper into strips, and cut the zucchini into rounds.
3. Toss all vegetables with olive oil and garlic powder in a large bowl.
4. Spread the vegetables evenly on a large baking sheet.
5. Pat the salmon fillet dry and season generously with salt, pepper, and fresh or dried dill.
6. Place the seasoned salmon on the baking sheet alongside the vegetables.
7. Roast everything in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes until the salmon flakes easily with a fork and reaches an internal temperature of 145°F.
8. Cut the lemon into wedges and serve alongside the salmon and vegetables.
9. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the salmon before eating.

Nutritional Information (per serving):
- Calories: 420 kcal
- Protein: 38g
- Carbohydrates: 18g
- Fat: 24g
- Fiber: 6g
- Sodium: 280mg

Dietary Information:
This recipe is suitable for: high-protein diets, low-carb diets, gluten-free diets, dairy-free diets, keto-friendly, paleo-friendly, pescatarian diets, omega-3 rich diets.
Allergens: Contains fish
This recipe is NOT suitable for: vegetarian, vegan diets, fish allergies.

Health Benefits:
- Extremely rich in omega-3 fatty acids (EPA and DHA) for heart and brain health
- Supports brain function and cognitive health
- Contains anti-inflammatory properties that may reduce chronic inflammation
- High in vitamin D from salmon
- Rich in B vitamins, particularly B12
- Provides selenium and potassium
- Low in carbohydrates, suitable for blood sugar management

Ingredient Substitutions:
- Replace salmon with cod, halibut, or arctic char
- Use asparagus instead of broccoli
- Substitute green beans for zucchini
- Add cherry tomatoes for extra flavor
- Use parsley or thyme instead of dill

Meal Timing: Suitable for lunch, dinner
Season: All seasons, especially spring and summer with fresh vegetables
